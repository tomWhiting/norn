Use to message another running agent. Address the recipient by hierarchical registry path (e.g. "/workers/analyzer"), UUID, or the literal "parent" for the agent that created you. The content field is a string; put structured payloads inside it as text or JSON — the message is delivered inside a harness-built frame either way.

Choose the kind deliberately. "steer" means act on this: it is injected at the recipient's next step boundary (immediately after its current tool batch) and wakes a recipient that is lingering at a stop boundary. "update" means FYI context: it batches with other updates and is injected when the recipient next reaches a boundary for its own reasons — it never wakes a lingering recipient. Redirections and corrections are steers; progress notes and shared findings are updates. One recipient is special: an interactive root (a human at a CLI or TUI) drains its inbound only when its operator runs a turn — a message to "parent" on such a surface is delivered into the next human-initiated step, not immediately, and a full parent channel makes your send wait until then.

Who you may message is fixed at your spawn time by your parent's grant and enforced against the registry: with scope "siblings_and_parent" you may message children of your own parent and your parent; with scope "parent_only" only your parent; a root agent may message its own children. The scope is always one hop — to reach an agent elsewhere in the tree, route through your parent and let each hop be audited. An out-of-scope recipient returns a structured permission failure naming your granted scope.

The message is delivered onto the recipient's inbound channel and injected as a harness-framed <agent_message> turn whose attribution (sender path, id, role, kind, sequence) is resolved by the runtime — message content cannot impersonate another sender. Messaging an agent that already finished fails with its recorded status and completion time — read its delivered result instead. A recipient with no live inbound route (its run just ended, or it is a root agent built without an inbound channel) also fails honestly; nothing is queued for later. If the recipient's channel is momentarily full, the send simply waits for space — it is never dropped. Check a recipient's status with the agents tool before retrying a failed send.

Every accepted send is recorded as an agent_message.sent audit event in your own session store and in your parent's store (so the agent that granted your scope sees the traffic without being on the data path); the recipient records agent_message.delivered plus the framed message itself at injection. The audit trail persists with the session; the in-flight channel does not — a sent-but-never-delivered message is detectable, not silent.
